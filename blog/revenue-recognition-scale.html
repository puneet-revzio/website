Revenue recognition at scale: why spreadsheets break at Series B

revzio Team · May 2026 · 7 min read

Series B is where revenue recognition stops being “a spreadsheet problem” and becomes a systems problem. The numbers don’t just get bigger — the shape of revenue changes. Contracts multiply, pricing becomes more complex, product expands, and finance inherits a flood of edge cases that don’t fit the neat rows you built at Series A.

The Series B inflection point: 50 contracts becomes 500

At 50 contracts, you can manually review each one. At 500, you can’t. You start sampling, which is where risk creeps in. Meanwhile, your billing system evolves: upgrades, downgrades, credits, usage overages, annual prepay, multiple performance obligations — and every change creates a recognition event.

Why Excel breaks (even with great spreadsheets)

Excel breaks because it wasn’t built to be a controlled system of record. Spreadsheets struggle with:

  • Manual schedules: they rely on humans to interpret contract events consistently.
  • No audit trail: version control is often “who saved the file last.”
  • Rework loops: when numbers change, your deck, schedules, and ERP entries all drift.
  • Hidden logic: formulas are easy to copy incorrectly and hard to validate.

The ASC 606 complexity that compounds it

ASC 606 is not just a standard — it’s a compounding system. Performance obligations, allocation, timing, contract modifications, and distinct goods/services all introduce branching logic. When those branches are implemented manually, consistency becomes impossible at scale.

How the Revenue Validation Agent handles recognition at scale
