3 weeks to go-live: how revzio onboards finance teams

revzio Team · May 2026 · 4 min read

Finance automation projects have a reputation: long timelines, IT tickets, brittle integrations, and a close team stuck in limbo. revzio’s onboarding model is different because Orion was built to plug into the finance stack you already run — ERP, billing, banking, and payroll — and because the agents start in a governed, human-in-the-loop mode.

Here’s what “go live in 3 weeks” actually looks like in practice.

Week 1: Connect your stack and configure agents

What happens

We connect your ERP and key data sources, normalize your chart of accounts context, and configure the first workflows. The goal isn’t “perfect automation” on day one — it’s getting a reliable data layer and clear policy gates for approvals.

Most teams start with Reconciliation, Journal Entries, and Close Management because those unlock immediate close-time wins without changing upstream systems.

Week 2: Observation mode and calibration

What happens

Orion agents run in parallel with your existing process. The Reconciliation Agent proposes matches and exceptions; the Journal Entry Agent drafts entries; the Accruals Agent generates schedules. Reviewers approve, override, and refine thresholds.

This is where the “finance-native configuration” matters: policy gates, tolerance thresholds, and reviewer routing — not prompt tweaking for a generic chatbot.

Week 3: Go live — exceptions only

What happens

Once reviewers are comfortable with accuracy and controls, Orion becomes the default workflow. Agents prepare the work continuously; humans handle exceptions and approvals. Close Management orchestrates tasks, dependencies, and sign-offs in product.

By the end of week three, teams typically see the close shift from “rebuilding the month” to “confirming what already happened.”

Why it’s fast

Speed comes from design choices: pre-built connectors, a unified data model, and workflows that assume governance (RBAC, audit trail, reviewer approvals) from day one. You’re not standing up a custom data warehouse or a brittle rules engine. You’re turning on agents that already understand finance’s constraints.
