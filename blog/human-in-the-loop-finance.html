What 'human-in-the-loop' actually means for finance teams

revzio Team · May 2026 · 5 min read

“Full automation” sounds great until you remember what finance is responsible for: accurate statements, defensible judgment calls, and an audit trail that survives scrutiny. In most organizations, the close isn’t slow because people are lazy — it’s slow because the work is high-stakes and error-prone. That’s why finance doesn’t need a black-box autopilot. It needs human-in-the-loop automation.

Why full automation is the wrong model for finance

Accounting is not a deterministic pipeline. Materiality thresholds change. Policy interpretation matters. New contracts introduce edge cases. And sometimes the “correct” answer depends on context that isn’t in any system field. If a system posts entries automatically with no review, the risk isn’t just a mistake — it’s a mistake that becomes institutionalized and repeated.

Auditors don’t just ask “is it right?” They ask “how do you know?” A human-in-the-loop model is the practical middle ground: agents do the prep work at machine speed; humans own approval and judgment; the audit trail is produced automatically.

What Orion automates vs what humans do

Orion agents automate the heavy lifting: ingesting messy data, normalizing it, proposing matches, generating journal entries, assembling schedules, and drafting variance explanations. That’s the time-consuming part humans shouldn’t be doing in spreadsheets.

Humans do what they’re uniquely good at: evaluating unusual exceptions, approving policy-sensitive actions, and making judgment calls on materiality. Orion makes that easier by surfacing evidence, rationale, and suggested next actions in plain English.

The three checkpoints Orion routes to humans

  • 1) Posting gates: when an agent prepares something that would write to the ERP (journal entries, adjustments), Orion routes it for approval first.
  • 2) Exception resolution: when matching isn’t high-confidence, Orion flags the break, explains why, and asks a reviewer to decide.
  • 3) Close sign-off: the Close Management Agent coordinates checklist completion and captures approver sign-off with evidence attached.

Why this model fits SOX and audit

Human-in-the-loop isn’t a buzzword — it’s a control design. It enforces segregation of duties, makes approvals explicit, and creates the audit trail automatically. Orion logs who reviewed what, when, and what evidence they saw. That’s how you scale automation without sacrificing governance.
