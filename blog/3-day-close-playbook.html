CLOSE AUTOMATION

From 10-day close to 3 days: the CFO playbook

revzio Team · May 2026 · 6 min read

The 10-day close persists in companies that have great people. That’s the uncomfortable truth. Most close delays aren’t a “work ethic” problem — they’re a systems problem: fragmented data, manual reconciliation, journal entries built from exports, and checklist coordination living in spreadsheets and Slack.

A three-day close is achievable when you compress the work that happens before month-end. Orion’s model is simple: agents prepare continuously; humans review and approve; audit trail is captured automatically.

Why the 10-day close persists

Traditional close processes are batch-driven. Teams wait for “final” bank files, “final” billing exports, “final” subledger updates — then scramble to reconcile everything at once. Exceptions are found late, owners are unclear, and the feedback loop is a week long.

Key idea: You don’t get a faster close by working harder in week one. You get it by moving reconciliation and prep work into every day.

The three phases Orion compresses

1) Reconciliation prep. The Reconciliation Agent matches transactions as they land, not after month-end. Breaks show up in hours, so owners can resolve them while context still exists.

2) Journal entry posting. The Journal Entry Agent generates entries from source data with traceability. The Accruals Agent automates schedules (prepaids, commissions, accruals). Your team reviews outputs instead of building spreadsheets.

3) Close checklist orchestration. The Close Management Agent coordinates tasks, enforces approvals, and captures sign-off with evidence. You stop asking “is this done?” because the system knows.

Month 1, 2, 3: what changes week-by-week

Month 1: Orion runs in observation mode alongside your current close. Agents ingest your ERP, billing, and banking sources. Finance sets policy gates: what can auto-match, what needs review, what must never auto-post.

Month 2: Daily matching becomes habit. Exceptions are routed to owners continuously. JEs are generated automatically and reviewed in smaller batches. Close checklist moves into product with dependencies and sign-offs.

Month 3: The close becomes confirmation. Reconciliation is already mostly complete. JEs are prepared. Evidence is attached. The only “fire drills” left are true anomalies.

A concrete example: deferred revenue reconciliation

One common bottleneck at Series B is deferred revenue. A team may spend two full days reconciling billing schedules to ERP postings because upgrades, credits, and timing differences accumulate. With Orion, those variances are flagged continuously and tied to the specific contract events that created them — so the month-end work is review, not investigation. Many teams see deferred revenue tie-outs go from two days to overnight.
