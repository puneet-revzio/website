The hidden cost of periodic close

Every month, the same pattern repeats: export from Stripe, pull the GL, build a reconciliation workbook, chase exceptions over Slack, and hope nothing material shows up on day four.

When matching only runs at month-end, variances compound quietly. A billing config change on the 3rd might not surface until the 28th.

What continuous reconciliation changes

Continuous close means reconciliation engines run as transactions land — with tolerances, rules, and explainability built in. Exceptions queue for owners while context is fresh.

Getting started without boiling the ocean

Start with your highest-variance stream. Run continuous matching in parallel with your existing process for one cycle. Most teams find quick wins before they retire the spreadsheet.

Three shifts that matter

  • Match at line-item level, not just invoice totals
  • Route exceptions with plain-English summaries
  • Keep an audit trail that does not depend on email
The goal is not more automation for its own sake — it is fewer surprises when the board asks why revenue moved.
