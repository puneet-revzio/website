Finance Automation Glossary

Plain-English definitions for CFOs, controllers, and finance teams navigating AI-powered workflows.

GLOSSARY

Definitions A–R.

When terms map directly to Orion, we call it out and link the relevant agent page.

A
Accrual Automation

Automating period-end accrual calculations and postings (e.g., payroll, commissions, prepaid amortization) from source data rather than spreadsheets. The goal is consistent schedules, fewer errors, and an audit-ready trail each close.

(→ Orion handles this) Accruals Agent
Agent Orchestration

The coordination layer that runs multiple specialized agents across a workflow, routes exceptions, and enforces approval steps. In finance, orchestration also means controls: policy gates, RBAC, and immutable audit logging.

(→ Orion handles this) How Orion Works
AP Automation

Automating invoice ingestion, extraction, approvals, and posting to ERP. Strong AP automation includes 3-way match, duplicate detection, and exception routing with context.

(→ Orion handles this) AP Agent
AR Automation

Automating cash application, collections prioritization, and dispute handling. The outcomes are lower DSO, fewer unmatched payments, and more predictable cash forecasting.

(→ Orion handles this) AR Agent
ASC 606

The revenue recognition standard for contracts with customers (US GAAP). It requires performance obligations, allocation, and timing rules that are hard to scale with spreadsheets as revenue complexity grows.

(→ Orion handles this) Revenue Validation Agent
Audit Trail

A record of who did what, when, and why — with supporting evidence. In modern finance automation, the audit trail is produced continuously as work is prepared and approved, not retroactively during PBC.

(→ Orion handles this) Controls & Audit Trails
B
Balance Sheet Reconciliation

The process of tying balance sheet accounts to subledgers, bank statements, and supporting schedules. Continuous balance sheet recon reduces late-cycle surprises and improves audit readiness.

(→ Orion handles this) Reconciliation Agent
Bank Reconciliation

Matching bank transactions to GL postings to ensure completeness and accuracy. Modern recon goes beyond totals to line-item and multi-source matching with explainable exceptions.

(→ Orion handles this) Reconciliation Agent
BPO (Business Process Outsourcing)

Outsourcing accounting operations to an external provider. BPO can reduce workload, but it often still relies on manual processes and doesn’t provide the continuous, in-product audit trail automation requires.

C
Cash Application

Matching incoming payments to open invoices using remittance details and bank references. High automation clears unmatched payments faster and improves DSO.

(→ Orion handles this) AR Agent
Close Calendar

The schedule of tasks, owners, and dependencies required to close the books. A strong close calendar tracks progress, routes approvals, and ties tasks to evidence.

(→ Orion handles this) Close Management Agent
Close Management Agent (Orion)

A specialized agent that orchestrates your close checklist, approvals, and variance summaries, capturing sign-off and evidence end-to-end. It replaces spreadsheets and “is this done?” threads with a governed workflow.

(→ Orion handles this) Close Management Agent
Continuous Close

A close model where reconciliation and prep work happen continuously, so month-end becomes confirmation instead of a scramble. Teams close faster because exceptions surface in hours, not week three.

(→ Orion handles this) Continuous Close
Controller

The finance leader responsible for accounting operations, controls, and the financial close. Controllers typically own reconciliation, journal entry review, and audit readiness.

D
DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)

A metric for how long it takes to collect revenue after a sale. AR automation improves DSO by matching cash faster and prioritizing collections by risk and value.

(→ Orion handles this) AR Agent
Data Ingestion Layer

The unified data layer that connects ERP, billing, banking, payroll, CRM, and warehouses and normalizes records into a single model. It’s the foundation every agent relies on for consistent outputs.

(→ Orion handles this) Orion Data Layer
Deferred Revenue

Revenue collected but not yet earned. Deferred revenue schedules can become complex with upgrades, credits, and usage billing — and need accurate posting and audit-ready schedules.

(→ Orion handles this) Revenue Validation Agent
E
ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)

A core system of record for accounting, finance, and operations (e.g., NetSuite, SAP). Orion integrates with ERPs to pull data and push outputs with governed approvals.

(→ Orion handles this) Integrations
Exception Management

The process of triaging breaks, variances, and mismatches that automation can’t resolve. Good exception management includes ranking by impact, routing to the right owner, and capturing evidence and outcomes.

(→ Orion handles this) Reconciliation Agent
F
Financial Close

The recurring process of finalizing financial statements for a period. It includes reconciliation, journal entries, accruals, approvals, and reporting.

(→ Orion handles this) Close Management Agent
Flux Analysis

Variance analysis that explains why balances changed between periods. In Orion, variance summaries can be drafted with source evidence and reviewer sign-off captured.

(→ Orion handles this) Close Management Agent
Three-Way Match (3-way match)

Validating an invoice by matching it to the purchase order (PO) and goods receipt (GRN). Automation reduces leakage by flagging duplicates and discrepancies immediately.

(→ Orion handles this) AP Agent
G
General Ledger (GL)

The accounting ledger where all transactions and adjustments are recorded. Reconciliation and journal automation ensure the GL reflects accurate, explainable activity.

Governance Controls

Policies, approvals, RBAC, and audit logging that ensure automation is safe and compliant. In finance-grade automation, controls are part of the workflow — not a manual afterthought.

(→ Orion handles this) Audit & Controls
H
Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)

A workflow design where automation prepares the work, but humans review and approve critical actions (like posting to ERP). HITL keeps judgment and accountability with finance teams.

(→ Orion handles this) How Orion Works
I
Invoice Processing

The end-to-end workflow for receiving, validating, approving, and posting invoices. Modern systems include extraction, 3-way match, duplicate detection, and routing.

(→ Orion handles this) AP Agent
Intercompany Reconciliation

Reconciling transactions between entities within the same company. It often requires multi-entity matching and consistent policies across ledgers and subledgers.

(→ Orion handles this) Reconciliation Agent
J
Journal Entry Automation

Generating and posting journal entries from source data automatically with validation and approvals. Strong automation includes lineage so auditors can trace every entry to evidence.

(→ Orion handles this) Journal Entry Agent
Journal Entry Agent (Orion)

A specialized agent that pulls from payroll, billing, banking, and ERP sources to generate journal entries, validate them against your chart of accounts, and route them to reviewers before posting.

(→ Orion handles this) Journal Entry Agent
L
LLM (Large Language Model) in Finance

A model that can generate and interpret language — used in finance to draft explanations, summarize exceptions, and translate messy operational data into reviewer-friendly context. In finance automation, LLM outputs must be governed, traceable, and reviewable.

(→ Orion handles this) How Orion Works
M
Month-End Close

The monthly cycle to finalize financial statements. Orion shifts the work earlier by running agents continuously and routing exceptions as they occur.

Multi-Entity Consolidation

Combining financials across subsidiaries or business units into consolidated statements. This requires consistent mappings, FX conversion, and intercompany reconciliation.

O
Orion Agent Suite

revzio’s suite of seven specialized finance agents that automate reconciliation, journal entries, AP, AR, accruals, revenue validation, and close management. Agents share a unified data layer and governed workflows.

(→ Orion handles this) Orion agent suite
Order-to-Cash (O2C)

The end-to-end process from invoice creation to cash collection and posting. In practice, it includes cash application, collections prioritization, and dispute routing.

(→ Orion handles this) Order to Cash
P
Prepaid Amortization

Expensing prepaid assets over time according to an amortization schedule. Automation reduces spreadsheet work and makes schedules audit-ready by default.

(→ Orion handles this) Accruals Agent
Procure-to-Pay (P2P)

The workflow from purchase order to invoice to payment. It includes 3-way match, approvals, duplicate detection, and ERP posting.

(→ Orion handles this) Procure to Pay
Policy-Gated Automation

Automation that runs within defined thresholds and routes exceptions to humans. This approach keeps finance in control while still capturing the bulk of the prep work automatically.

(→ Orion handles this) How Orion Works
R
Reconciliation Agent (Orion)

A specialized agent that matches transactions across bank, GL, and subledger using scalable rules, flags breaks with explanations, and logs evidence for audit. It supports one-to-one, one-to-many, and many-to-many matching.

(→ Orion handles this) Reconciliation Agent
Record-to-Report (R2R)

The accounting cycle that turns raw transactions into closed books and reports: reconciliation, journal entries, accruals, review, and sign-off.

(→ Orion handles this) Record to Report
Revenue Recognition

Determining when and how revenue is recognized in financial statements, often governed by ASC 606 for customer contracts. Complex billing models increase the chance of drift between billing and ERP without continuous validation.

(→ Orion handles this) Revenue Validation Agent
RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)

A security model where permissions are assigned by role (e.g., preparer, reviewer, approver). RBAC supports segregation of duties and makes approvals enforceable by the system.

(→ Orion handles this) Security
